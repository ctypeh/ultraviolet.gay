Developing

I develop and scan my photos with the lovely Panda Lab in Seattle, Washington, because film is interesting, but scanning it is not. Shooting film instead of digital gives me a great reason to play around with some of the best stim toys I've ever found and look at bright lights and colors wherever I am all day, without sitting in front of a garbage device that makes an insufferably high-pitched whine and takes forever. Plus+, Sarah drives me all the way over there to the Space Needle on her motorcycle with the canisters tucked into my jacket pocket, which makes me feel really cool and sexy.

135

I shoot with a Nikon F, Praktica B1/200, and a Rollei 35 T.

I need a list somewhere to remember what lenses I've already bought, so here is that list: For the Praktica, I have f2.4 f1.8 50mm, f2.8 28mm, f2.8 125mm Pentacon Prakticar lenses, and a Carl Zeiss Jena f4 300mm lens, because I am ideologically pure. I've also enjoyed playing around with macro-extension tubes and a Rollei 121BC flash.

I'm increasingly doing my own camera repair and, as a direct result, also now have a working B200.

Film

Trying to shoot black and white film when I started is a funny mistake to make for someone who cares as much about color and light as I do. Since starting to to shoot color, I've had a much better time. I don't like Portra. Other than that, I lived in Rochester when I was younger, so they're OK with me. Ektar is awesome. CineStill seems pretty cool, I like 800T so far.

120

Sometimes Sarah lets me shoot with her RolleiFlex, so that I can take square photos instead of rectangular photos. I think it's an Automat? It has a 70mm Tessar lens, if I remember correctly.

Editing

I don't really understand how, so most of these are all straight out of the camera. If you wanna teach me how, go ahead, but I'm having a great time regardless.
I like the intrinsic properties of film—Halation is awesome–as much as I do working within the limits of my camera, and getting a feel for the color profile of each film stock I try. I'm not looking for a perfect image, just having some fun and finding some beautiful things along the way.

Unrelatedly, I owe this web page an enormous thank you, so I'm taking this space to do so. Now that I've put it here, I won't lose it when I need it later.
